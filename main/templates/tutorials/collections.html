Collections in World Historical Gazetteer

Collections in WHG are sets of datasets. Registered users can link datsets they own (or co-own), for purposes of presentation and filtering of searches. In a future release, it will be possible to create collections from individual records and/or search results.

Why collections?

Creators of gazetteers for a particular region and period are increasingly interested in linking their data with projects that have overlapping spatial and/or temporal coverage. Loosely joining datasets in the WHG platform can potentially generate rich domains of focus, effectively combining the efforts of numerous specialized projects.

For example, the KNAW/HuC group in the Netherlands has begun developing a collection that will ultimately link a few dozen datasets of settlements and administrative areas in historically Dutch territories around the globe.

Preliminary discussion have begin on several similar efforts, including for Colonial and pre-Colonial Latin America (LatAm), for the Ottoman Empire, for Central Eurasia, for Early Modern Europe, and for the geography of the Atlantic slave trade.

Collections can also be used to link datasets from a single contributor, as Werner Stangl has done with HGIS de las Indias. One dataset contains around 13,000 settlements (lugares), and another contains the 900 political and religious administrative areas (territorios) they lay within.
